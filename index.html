Track record · how the model is doing

Player stats. All 48 teams are loaded with two key players each. Numbers are the 2025-26 club season (all competitions) unless labelled otherwise — "Intl" is international career, "Caps" is international appearances. For some smaller nations a clean season figure wasn't reliably available, so the chip shows role/club instead of a fabricated number. Compiled from public previews and fully editable.

How to read this. Every team now carries its own attack and defence rating. A side's expected goals = its attack against the other side's defence, so the total shifts with the matchup — a sharp attack meeting a leaky defence runs hot, two strong defences run cold — instead of every game sharing one fixed total. Both ratings start from the team's overall strength, tilted by a light, editable style prior (Morocco and Iran lean defensive, Norway and Germany lean attacking), and you can set them by hand under “Adjust ratings & form.” Those two expected-goal figures feed a Dixon-Coles Poisson model for every scoreline, which drives the win odds, markets and EV. Ratings are current as of 11 Jun 2026 — verified for the top tier, estimated below it.

Honest limits. This is a strength-based statistical estimate. It does not know lineups, injuries, suspensions, or weather. Bookmaker odds fold all of that in, so expect differences — use the form sliders to nudge for team news, and treat the output as one well-reasoned view, not a guarantee.
